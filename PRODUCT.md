# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Current students and graduates of Tribhuvan University’s Institute of Engineering, especially students navigating course requirements, preparing for exams, planning study, finding trustworthy resources, or translating their academic record for opportunities abroad. They may use the product on laptops or phones, under time pressure, and with limited familiarity with formal academic planning tools.

## Product Purpose

IOE Student Utils is an open-source, community-driven academic toolkit that turns fragmented course, institution, scholarship, and application information into clear student workflows. Success means a student can understand what to learn, decide what to do next, find the right supporting material, and move between tools without needing to understand the system’s internal data model.

## Brand Personality

Practical, reassuring, and student-led. The interface should feel credible enough for consequential academic decisions, approachable enough for first-time users, and direct rather than promotional.

## Anti-references

- Marketing-first product pages that delay the student’s task with oversized hero sections or decorative metrics.
- Visualizations that are impressive at first glance but harder to scan than a structured syllabus.
- Fragmented dashboards that expose database concepts, opaque identifiers, or competing navigation paths.
- Generic card grids that give every item equal visual weight and make academic hierarchy difficult to perceive.
- Dense institutional portals that assume prior knowledge and provide weak mobile or keyboard access.

## Design Principles

1. Start with the student’s next decision, not the underlying data model.
2. Reveal course depth progressively, from program and semester to subject, unit, topic, and resource.
3. Keep familiar academic structure visible while using graphs only when relationships add real understanding.
4. Use human-readable names and stable slugs at every user-facing boundary.
5. Preserve context across tools so exploring, studying, and planning feel like one learning journey.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All primary workflows must work with keyboard and screen readers, support text resizing and reduced motion, avoid color-only meaning, provide a non-graph alternative to relationship visualizations, and remain usable across mobile screens and constrained network conditions.
